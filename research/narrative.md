# Campaign Narrative

## The n=26 Circle Packing: From Search to Proof

The campaign began with a simple question: what is the maximum sum of radii when packing 26 circles into the unit square? Wave 1 deployed 15 optimizer variants -- basin-hopping, CMA-ES, differential evolution, JAX soft-body annealing, Mobius deformations, genetic topology search, and more -- all converging to the same packing with sum_r = 2.6359830865. The orbit mobius-001 emerged as the winner, establishing a solution that beats the prior SOTA of 2.635 by +0.00098. Exhaustive topology search across 8000+ configurations found no alternative basin, suggesting this is the global optimum.

Wave 2 shifted the campaign from empirical search to mathematical certification. Two independent rigorous proofs now confirm the solution is a strict isolated local maximum: rigidity-001 computed sigma_min(R) = 0.0907 for the 78x78 rigidity matrix (five orders of magnitude above the degeneracy threshold), while interval-newton-001 applied the Krawczyk operator to prove a unique KKT critical point exists within a ball of radius 10^-4 around the numerical solution -- a computer-verified theorem. Contact-graph-001 enumerated 2100 structured topology alternatives (edge-flip, wall-swap, 2-edge-swap) and found zero improvements, closing the combinatorial neighborhood. Meanwhile, two attempts to tighten the upper bound via polynomial relaxations (sparse SDP and Krivine-Handelman LP) both failed decisively, validating the brainstorm panel's prediction that algebraic tools cannot match geometric covering arguments for this problem class.

The campaign now stands at a crossroads. The local question is settled: 2.6359830865 is rigorously the unique local maximum in its topological neighborhood, certified by two independent mathematical frameworks. The global question remains open: a 3.9% gap persists between the solution and the Fejes-Toth upper bound of 2.7396. The remaining paths forward are geometric (Voronoi-cell boundary-corrected bounds), probabilistic (Ginibre-ensemble bias-breaking restarts to address Connelly's 60/40 dissent on global optimality), and combinatorial (broader contact-graph enumeration via homotopy continuation -- a months-long project). The thesis has strengthened from "empirically saturated" to "rigorously certified local optimum with combinatorial neighborhood exhausted."
